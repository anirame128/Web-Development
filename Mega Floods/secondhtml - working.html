The Mystery of Mega Floods

Mega floods have taken place all around the globe. They have used their powerful force to shape the landscapes we see today. Although not much there creation is known,until now. Sientist are using new ways to find out how these dramatic landscapes formed.

The scablands, one of the many extraordinary landscapes that have been changed by the violent forces of a mega flood. The scablands formation puzzled scientists for many years. The dramatic cliffs and mountains were then found out to have been the result of a mega flood. While researching scientists found one clue that brought them to the idea of a flood. Most cliffs that have been carved or eroded by water will have the shape of a horseshoe. One of the most known waterfalls in the world niagara falls has the same shape as cliffs in the scablands. For that reason this part of the scablands is called the dry falls. They are five times the size of niagara and almost twice as tall.

Another clue also lies here in the scablands. Many miles away from the drie falls are strange super sized sinkholes. Sinkholes are many times found under fast going rivers. This can only point to one thing that a giant force of water carved this ripped apart landscape. Something very mysterious is that there is not a single lake or river the size and proximity away from this area to suit a flood like this. To get this type of formation a huge amount of water must be in the area.

To answer this peculiar question scientists investigate a different landscape three and a half thousand miles away in Iceland. This island is a place of fire and ice. In the northeast corner huge scars similar to those of the scablands are found. This is the Asbyrgi canyon. Like in the scablands scientists want to know how this similar landscape was created. They are so similar that people suspect they were made in the same way. To find out how long it took to carve these magnificent landscapes scientists are using a method called surface exposure dating. Earth's surface rocks are constantly bombarded by cosmic rays from space. The rocks under the first layer are protected from these rays but when the first layer is eroded the bottom rocks are now under attack.
